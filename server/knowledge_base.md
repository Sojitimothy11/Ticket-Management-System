## Account & Login Issues

### Q1: I forgot my password. What should I do?

1.  Go to the login page.
2.  Click **Forgot Password**.
3.  Enter your registered email address.
4.  Follow the instructions in the reset email.

If you do not receive the email, check your spam or promotions folder.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Q2: I'm not receiving the password reset email.

Possible reasons: - The email was entered incorrectly. - The account was
created using a different email. - The email is in your spam folder.

If the email does not arrive within 10 minutes, contact support.

## General CRM questions

### Q11. What is a CRM system?
A CRM (Customer Relationship Management) system is software that helps a business store customer data, track interactions, and manage sales, marketing, and support activities in one place.

### Q12. Why does my business need a CRM?
A CRM helps centralise customer information, improve team collaboration, automate follow-ups, and provide reports that support better decisions and revenue growth.

### Q13. What are the main benefits of using a CRM?
Typical benefits include better visibility of the sales pipeline, improved customer service, fewer data silos, more accurate reporting, and faster response times to customer enquiries.

### Q14. What types of CRM systems exist?
Most vendors describe three types: operational CRM, analytical CRM, and collaborative CRM.

### Q15. How is CRM different from ERP?
CRM focuses on managing customer relationships and revenue-generating activities, while ERP focuses on internal operations such as finance, inventory, and supply chain.

## Data and records

### Q16. What is the difference between a lead, a contact, and an account?
A lead is an unqualified prospect, a contact is a known person, and an account is the organisation or customer record that contacts belong to.

### Q17. What information should I store for each customer?
At minimum, store name, company, contact details, communication preferences, relationship owner, key interactions, and any agreements such as contracts or renewals.

### Q18. How do I keep my CRM data clean?
Use required fields, validation rules, duplicate detection, standard picklists, and regular data reviews so that records stay accurate and consistent.

### Q19. How often should customer records be updated?
Update records after every meaningful interaction and review key accounts on a regular schedule, such as monthly or quarterly, depending on account size.

## Sales and pipeline

### Q20. What is a sales pipeline?
A sales pipeline is a visual representation of opportunities moving through defined stages from initial contact to closed won or lost.

### Q21. Which sales metrics should I track in the CRM?
Common metrics include number of new leads, lead-to-opportunity conversion rate, pipeline value by stage, win rate, and average sales cycle length.

### Q22. How can a CRM help my sales team close more deals?
A CRM supports better prioritisation of deals, timely follow-ups, clear visibility of next steps, and collaboration around complex opportunities.

## Customer service and success

### Q23. How does a CRM improve customer service?
CRM software gives agents a full history of each customer, making it easier to respond quickly, personalise answers, and track open issues through to resolution.

### Q24. What customer service metrics should we measure?
Key metrics include first response time, average handle time, resolution time, customer satisfaction score, and number of open cases by priority.

### Q25. Can a CRM help reduce churn?
Yes, by tracking renewals, health scores, and risk indicators, a CRM helps customer success teams intervene early and run structured retention and upsell programmes.

## AI assistant and automation

### Q26. How can an AI assistant use our CRM data?
An AI assistant can read knowledge base articles, past tickets, and CRM data to answer common questions, suggest next best actions, and draft responses for agents.

### Q27. What kind of questions can an AI assistant handle?
It can handle repetitive queries such as order status, basic product information, opening hours, pricing tiers, and how-to steps taken from your knowledge base and CRM.

### Q28. How do we train the AI assistant on our knowledge base?
Prepare clean FAQ articles, standard operating procedures, and troubleshooting guides, then connect the assistant to those sources and review its answers regularly to correct and improve them.

### Q29. Will the AI assistant replace human agents?
The AI assistant is best used to handle simple, high-volume questions so that human agents can focus on complex or sensitive issues that require judgment and empathy.

### Q30. How do we measure if the AI assistant is working well?
Track containment rate, customer satisfaction, average resolution time, and the percentage of conversations that must be handed off to a human agent.

## Implementation and adoption

### Q31. How do we choose the right CRM?
Evaluate ease of use, integration options, reporting capabilities, scalability, total cost of ownership, and the level of support the vendor provides.

### Q32. How long does CRM implementation usually take?
Implementation time depends on complexity, but small teams often go live within a few weeks, while larger, integrated deployments can take several months.

### Q33. How can we encourage the team to use the CRM consistently?
Provide clear processes, easy-to-follow training, helpful dashboards, and leadership support, and avoid making the system unnecessarily complicated.

### Q34. What are common mistakes when adopting a CRM?
Typical mistakes include not involving end users, over-customising too early, ignoring data quality, and failing to define clear goals and success metrics.
